Unbelievably, there are over 50 different kinds of nuts in the globe, yet only around 20 of them are thought to be safe for humans to eat.
All things considered, nuts are a very nutrient-dense diet with some incredible—yet usually disregarded—health advantages. They are high in protein, satisfying, and a fantastic source of good plant-based fats.
Learn more about the health benefits of nuts and discover the top ten nuts for your diet.
The 11 common types of culinary nuts—often used for snacking, baking, and cooking—are almonds, Brazil nuts, cashews, chestnuts, hazelnuts, macadamia nuts, peanuts (technically a legume), pecans, pine nuts, pistachios, and walnuts. These are popular for their rich flavor profiles and health benefits, including protein, healthy fats, and fiber.
Walnuts, almonds, pistachios, and cashews are excellent dry fruits for heart health, as they are packed with omega-3 fatty acids, healthy monounsaturated fats, fiber, and antioxidants that lower bad cholesterol (LDL) and reduce inflammation. Daily consumption of a small handful (20-30g) of unsalted nuts and dried fruits is recommended.
